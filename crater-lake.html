Your Adventure

Two Days at
the Crater

A perfectly paced itinerary balancing iconic viewpoints, gentle hikes, and unhurried mornings. Tap any activity for details.

🌅
8:00 AM
Rim Village — First Glimpse
Viewpoint
Step out at Rim Village for your first look at the crater — the blue is genuinely shocking. Grab a coffee at the Rim Village Café before the day's crowds arrive. The morning light on the caldera walls is extraordinary. No hike needed; pure wonder.
📍 Apple Maps ℹ️ NPS Info

9:00 AM
Discovery Point Trail
Easy · 2.6 mi RT
A nearly flat rim trail heading west from Rim Village with front-row views of Wizard Island and the full caldera. The path hugs the crater edge the entire way with incredible panoramas. Minimal elevation gain — a perfect warm-up. Allow 1.5 hrs.
📍 Apple Maps 🗺️ AllTrails
🍽️
12:00 PM
Lunch · Crater Lake Lodge
Dining · $$$
The historic 1915 lodge dining room sits directly on the rim with floor-to-ceiling windows over the caldera. The seasonal menu always includes thoughtful vegetarian entrées. Request a window table when booking — this one is worth reserving in advance.
📍 Apple Maps 🔗 Reserve Table
☁️
2:00 PM
Cloudcap Overlook
Highest Point · Drive-to
At 7,960 ft, Cloudcap is the highest accessible point on the Rim Drive. Pull over and take in the sweeping eastern panorama — the full caldera, Mount Scott, and distant peaks. No hiking required; this is pure drama by car.
📍 Apple Maps
⛵
3:30 PM
Phantom Ship Overlook
Viewpoint
Pull over on the south rim for a jaw-dropping view of Phantom Ship — a dark volcanic island that looks like a ghost galleon sailing through electric-blue water. The contrast of dark rock against vivid blue is unlike anything else in the park.
📍 Apple Maps
🏔️
4:30 PM
Watchman Peak Trail
Moderate · 1.6 mi RT
A short but thrilling hike to the historic Watchman Fire Lookout perched at 8,013 ft. 360° panoramas of the full crater, Wizard Island directly below, and Mount McLoughlin in the distance. Gain 420 ft over 0.8 miles. Allow 60–75 min round trip.
📍 Apple Maps 🗺️ AllTrails
🌙
7:00 PM
Dinner + Sunset at Rim Village
Dining · Sunset
Dine at Annie Creek Restaurant in Mazama Village, then return to the rim for one of the most spectacular sunsets you will ever see. The alpenglow turns the caldera walls from gold to violet. Stay late — Crater Lake is a Dark Sky park and the stargazing is surreal.
📍 Annie Creek 🌅 Rim Village
🌄
6:30 AM
Sunrise at Rim Village
Sunrise
Set your alarm. Crater Lake at sunrise belongs to almost no one — still, silver, and otherworldly before the day's visitors arrive. The lake transitions from pale silver to deep cobalt as the sun clears the eastern rim. Bring a warm layer; mornings run cold even in July.
📍 Apple Maps

9:00 AM
Cleetwood Cove Trail
Moderate · 2.2 mi RT
The only trail that descends all the way to the lake shore — an absolute must-do. The path drops 700 ft in 1.1 miles, so the return climb earns its "moderate" rating. At the bottom, touch the crystalline water. This trailhead is also where boat tours depart.
📍 Apple Maps 🗺️ AllTrails
🏝️
11:00 AM
Wizard Island Boat Tour
Optional · 2–3 hrs
Ranger-led boat tours depart from Cleetwood Cove to Wizard Island — the volcanic cinder cone rising from the lake. You can swim off the rocks (bracing!), hike the island's summit crater trail, or just marvel at being on the water. Book months ahead; they sell out fast.

1:00 PM
Drive to Ashland for Lunch
75 mi south
Head south to Ashland, OR — Southern Oregon's culinary gem and the best food town near the park. The scenic 90-minute drive passes through stunning high-desert terrain. Ashland's vibrant vegetarian dining scene makes the drive completely worth it.

3:30 PM
Garfield Peak Trail
Moderate · 3.4 mi RT
Widely considered the best hike in the park. A steady climb from Crater Lake Lodge to Garfield Peak at 8,054 ft, with nearly the entire caldera visible from the summit. Wildflowers line the trail in July and August. Allow 2.5–3 hours; 1,010 ft elevation gain.

5:30 PM
Godfrey Glen Loop
Easy · 1 mi loop
Wind down with this peaceful 1-mile loop through an old-growth forest with dramatic canyon views of Munson Creek below. A complete contrast to the rim — cool, shaded, cathedral-quiet. The perfect counterpoint to two days of epic views. Takes about 30 minutes.
📍 Apple Maps 🗺️ AllTrails

7:30 PM
Farewell Dinner in Ashland
Fine Dining
End your Crater Lake chapter with a memorable dinner in downtown Ashland. The town's farm-to-table scene punches well above its size. Larks or the Standing Stone Brewing Company are perfect send-offs before your drive home.

Good to Know

Before You Go

A few things that will make the trip smoother and safer.

🎫
Park Entry
$35 per vehicle (7-day pass). The America the Beautiful Annual Pass ($80) covers all national parks — great value if you visit 3+ parks a year.
🌡️
Layer Up
The rim sits at 7,100 ft. Summer mornings are often below 50°F and wind at the crater edge is fierce. A fleece and windbreaker are essential year-round.
💧
Hydrate
Altitude dehydrates faster than you expect. Carry 2+ liters per person for any hike. Water refill stations at Rim Village and Mazama Village.
🅿️
Arrive Early
Rim Village parking fills by 9 AM in peak season (July–Sept). Arriving by 7–8 AM secures a spot and rewards you with crowd-free solitude.
📶
No Cell Service
Download offline maps before arriving. AllTrails, Google Maps, and Maps.me all work offline. Download hike routes at your accommodation the night before.
🌙
Stay for Stars
Crater Lake is a designated Dark Sky park. On clear nights, the Milky Way reflects in the water. It may be the most beautiful thing you have ever seen.
